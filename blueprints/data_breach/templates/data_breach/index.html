Frequently Asked Questions

What is a data breach?

A data breach occurs when sensitive, protected or confidential data is copied, transmitted, viewed, stolen or used by an individual unauthorized to do so. This can include personal information, login credentials, financial data, and more.

How do you find data breaches?

We use multiple APIs to search known data breach repositories. Our service queries HaveIBeenPwned, BreachDirectory, DeHashed, and other databases to provide a comprehensive view of where your information may have been exposed.

What should I do if my data is found in a breach?

If your data is found in a breach, you should:

  • Change your password for the affected service immediately
  • Change passwords on other services where you use the same or similar password
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity

Is my search information saved?

By default, we do not store the email addresses or domains you search for. Your search data is only used to query the breach databases and is not saved or shared with third parties.
